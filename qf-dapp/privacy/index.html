Privacy Notice — Competitive Games

Last updated: March 2026

This notice covers the MathsWins competitive games platform at /dapp/, where players pay QF tokens to compete for weekly prize pots. The main MathsWins site has a separate privacy notice.

What We Collect

Data Why Stored Where
Wallet address To record entries, attribute scores, and distribute prizes Server database
Game scores Leaderboard rankings and prize eligibility Server database
Session data (game played, attempt number, timestamp) Fair play enforcement and preventing duplicate entries Server database
Entry history (game, tier, week, transaction hash) Verifying payments and settlement records Server database
Settlement records Prize distribution audit trail Server database
Analytics (pages visited, traffic source, device type, region) To understand which games are popular Google Analytics (cookieless)

What We Don't Collect

  • Your name, email, or any personal identity — you connect with a wallet, nothing more
  • Your wallet balance or transaction history beyond entries on this platform
  • Tracking cookies — analytics run in cookieless mode
  • Private keys — we never have access to your wallet

On-Chain Data

When you enter a game, your wallet address and entry details are recorded on the QF Network blockchain. Blockchain transactions are public and permanent by design — this is not something we control. The smart contracts involved are:

Network: QF Network (Chain ID 3426)
GameEntry: 0x6e1d573A8e40BaCb2ccC0A913a2989e35bE1151d
PrizePot: 0x8A433d114Da837f99c62B391d770523F2Bb9fD0F

On-chain data (entry events, prize distributions, token burns) is publicly visible to anyone. Our server database stores the same information off-chain for leaderboard display and settlement processing.

Wallet Addresses as Personal Data

A wallet address on its own doesn't identify you. However, under UK GDPR, if a wallet address could be linked to an identifiable person (for example, if you've publicly associated your address with your identity elsewhere), it may be considered personal data. We treat wallet addresses with the same care as personal data for this reason.

Cookies

The dapp uses no cookies. Authentication is handled by your wallet connection (a direct browser-to-blockchain interaction). Game sessions use JWT tokens that are not stored on your device between visits. Analytics run in cookieless mode.

Lawful Basis

We process data under legitimate interests: running competitive games with prize pots requires recording who entered, what they scored, and who won. Without this data, we cannot operate the platform, distribute prizes, or maintain fair play.

How Long We Keep It

Data Retention
Scores and leaderboard data Indefinitely (historical leaderboards)
Entry and settlement records Indefinitely (financial audit trail)
Session data 90 days after the relevant game week ends
Google Analytics data 14 months (Google's default)
On-chain data Permanent (blockchain is immutable)

If you want your off-chain data removed, email us. We can delete your scores and session data from our database. We cannot delete on-chain transaction records — no one can.

Who Sees Your Data

  • Public leaderboard — your wallet address and scores are displayed on the leaderboard (this is the point of a competitive platform)
  • Google — cookieless analytics only
  • Cloudflare — API tunnel to our backend
  • QF Network — on-chain entry and settlement transactions are publicly visible

We do not sell or share your data with anyone else. There is no advertising on the platform.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the off-chain data we hold about your wallet address
  • Request deletion of off-chain data (scores, sessions)
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Lodge a complaint with the ICO (ico.org.uk)

Note: we cannot modify or delete on-chain data. Blockchain transactions are permanent and outside anyone's control once confirmed.

To exercise any of these rights, email contact@mathswins.co.uk. We'll respond within 30 days.

Changes to This Notice

If we change what data we collect or how we use it, we'll update this page and the date at the top.
