Poker is the only common casino/card game where the house doesn't have an edge against you — you're playing against other humans. The house takes a small rake (the small fee the casino takes from each pot), but your opponent's mistakes are your profit. The maths isn't about beating the casino. It's about being better than the other players at the table.

POKER VS BLACKJACK: THE KEY DIFFERENCE

In blackjack, perfect play reduces the house edge to 0.5% — but you still lose long-term. In poker, there is no house edge. You play against other people. If you make better mathematical decisions than your opponents, you win their money. The rake is the cost of playing — typically 2.5-5% of each pot, capped.

This school teaches you to make better mathematical decisions than 90% of recreational players. That's the edge. That's the profit.
