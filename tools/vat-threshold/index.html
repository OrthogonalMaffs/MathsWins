UK VAT RULES — THE NUMBERS

The Threshold

You must register for VAT if your taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in any rolling 12-month period, or if you expect it to exceed £90,000 in the next 30 days alone. This is not based on your tax year or calendar year — HMRC looks at any consecutive 12-month window.

Registration Deadline

You must notify HMRC within 30 days of the end of the month in which you crossed the threshold. Your VAT registration is backdated to the first day of the second month after you crossed. Miss it and HMRC can charge you the VAT you should have been collecting.

Crossed threshold on 15 July → Notify by 30 August → VAT registered from 1 August

De-registration

If your turnover drops below £88,000, you can apply to de-register. Note this is £2,000 below the registration threshold — a buffer to prevent businesses repeatedly registering and de-registering.

What VAT Actually Costs You

VAT at 20% does not mean your costs increase by 20%. You reclaim VAT on your business expenses (inputs), so you only pay the difference between VAT collected and VAT reclaimed. If your expenses are 30% of turnover, the effective cost is closer to 14%.

Effective VAT cost = 20% × (1 − expense ratio)

Quarterly Returns

Standard VAT returns are quarterly. You can opt for monthly returns if you regularly reclaim more than you owe (common in capital-intensive businesses). Each return is due one month and seven days after the end of the VAT period.

VAT registration is not optional above the threshold. If you are approaching £90,000, start planning now — adjust your pricing, understand the Flat Rate Scheme, and speak to an accountant before you cross.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

When do I need to register for VAT?

When your taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in any rolling 12-month period, or you expect it to exceed £90,000 in the next 30 days. You must register within 30 days of the end of the month in which you crossed the threshold.

Can I voluntarily register below the threshold?

Yes. If your customers are VAT-registered businesses, they can reclaim your VAT so it doesn't increase their costs. You benefit by reclaiming VAT on your own expenses. Voluntary registration can save you money if your input VAT is significant.

What is the Flat Rate Scheme?

Instead of tracking VAT on every purchase and sale, you pay a fixed percentage of turnover (rate depends on your trade). Simpler admin but sometimes more expensive — this calculator compares both approaches for your numbers.

Will VAT registration lose me customers?

If your customers are consumers (B2C), yes — your prices effectively increase by up to 20%. If they're businesses (B2B), no — they reclaim the VAT. Know your customer base before you panic.

What happens if I don't register in time?

HMRC can backdate your registration and charge you VAT on sales from the date you should have registered. Plus penalties and interest. Don't miss the deadline.
