HOW TAX-FREE CHILDCARE WORKS

The 20% Top-Up

Tax-Free Childcare is a government scheme that adds 20% to the money you put into a special childcare account. For every £8 you deposit, the government adds £2. The maximum government contribution is £2,000 per child per year (£500 per quarter), which means the scheme tops up your first £10,000 of annual childcare spending per child.

For every £8 you pay in → Government adds £2 → Maximum £2,000/child/year

Who Is Eligible?

Both parents (or a single parent) must earn at least £8,670 per year — that is equivalent to 16 hours per week at the National Minimum Wage. Neither parent can earn more than £100,000. You must be employed or self-employed. If you are on certain benefits (like disability benefits), different rules may apply.

Your child must be under 12 years old, or under 17 if they have a disability. For disabled children, the maximum government top-up doubles to £4,000 per year.

Free Hours Entitlements

On top of Tax-Free Childcare, the government provides free childcare hours. These are term-time only (38 weeks per year), though some providers let you "stretch" them across more weeks at fewer hours per week. Use your free hours first, then Tax-Free Childcare for the remaining cost.

Combining Free Hours + Tax-Free Childcare

This is where the real savings stack up. A 3-year-old getting 30 free hours could save you roughly £15,000 per year in free hours alone. On top of that, Tax-Free Childcare can save another £2,000 on any remaining costs. This calculator shows you the full picture.

Tax-Free Childcare is not a tax deduction — it is free money deposited directly into your childcare account. You do not need to do a tax return to claim it. Register at childcare.service.gov.uk and reconfirm every 3 months.

Childcare Vouchers vs Tax-Free Childcare

Childcare vouchers closed to new entrants in September 2018. If you are still on the scheme, you can continue using them, but you cannot use both vouchers and Tax-Free Childcare at the same time. For most people, Tax-Free Childcare gives a bigger saving — especially higher-rate taxpayers who were limited to £124/month in vouchers (saving £993/year) versus £2,000/year with Tax-Free Childcare.

Basic-rate taxpayers saved up to £933/year with vouchers vs £2,000/year with Tax-Free Childcare. The comparison table in this calculator shows both options side by side.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is Tax-Free Childcare?

A government scheme where for every £8 you pay into a childcare account, the government adds £2. That's a 20% saving, up to £2,000 per child per year. You pay your childcare provider from this account.

Am I eligible for Tax-Free Childcare?

Both parents must earn at least £8,670/year and neither can earn over £100,000. You must be working (employed or self-employed). Single parents just need to meet the income requirements themselves.

How do I sign up?

Register at childcare.service.gov.uk. You'll need to reconfirm eligibility every 3 months. Your childcare provider must also be registered with the scheme.

Can I use Tax-Free Childcare AND free hours?

Yes! Use your 15 or 30 free hours first, then Tax-Free Childcare for any remaining costs. They work together.

What about childcare vouchers?

Childcare vouchers closed to new members in September 2018. If you're still on them, this calculator shows which scheme saves more. For most people, Tax-Free Childcare is better.

Is Tax-Free Childcare better than Universal Credit childcare?

You can't use both. Universal Credit covers up to 85% of childcare costs (max £951/month for one child). If you're on UC, that's usually more generous. This calculator compares your options.
