THE MATHEMATICS
Monthly payment formula: M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1] where: M = monthly payment P = principal (outstanding balance) r = monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12) n = number of monthly payments (years × 12) Every pound of overpayment reduces P directly. Since interest = P × r each month, reducing P means less interest next month, more of your payment goes to principal, which reduces P further. This is a positive feedback loop — the more you overpay, the faster the balance drops, the less interest accrues, the faster the balance drops further.
Overpaying a mortgage is one of the highest guaranteed "returns" available. If your rate is 4.5%, every pound of overpayment saves you 4.5% per year in interest — tax-free, risk-free, guaranteed. No savings account matches that after tax.
