THE MATHS BEHIND FREE BETS

A free bet is not free money. Its real value depends on the type of free bet, the odds you use it on, and whether you have access to a betting exchange.

SNR FREE BETS (STAKE NOT RETURNED)

Most free bets are SNR — if you win, you receive the profit but NOT the stake back. This means the free bet is always worth less than its face value.

Value = Face Value × (Odds - 1) / Odds £20 free bet at 2.00: £20 × 1/2 = £10.00 (50%) £20 free bet at 3.00: £20 × 2/3 = £13.33 (67%) £20 free bet at 5.00: £20 × 4/5 = £16.00 (80%) £20 free bet at 10.00: £20 × 9/10 = £18.00 (90%)
Higher odds give you more value from an SNR free bet. This is the opposite of normal betting, where shorter odds have lower overround. For free bets, back the longshot.

SR FREE BETS (STAKE RETURNED)

Rare but golden. An SR free bet returns the stake with your winnings. Mathematically, it's worth 100% of its face value at any odds.

SR free bet EV = Face Value × P(win) × Odds / 1 = Face Value (at any odds) Proof: At odds d, probability of winning ≈ 1/d EV = (1/d) × d × Face Value = Face Value ✓
SR free bets are worth their full face value. Use them on the shortest odds you can find to minimise variance — you want the guaranteed money, not the excitement.

LAY-OFF FOR GUARANTEED PROFIT

If you have access to a betting exchange, you can convert expected value into guaranteed profit. Back with the free bet at the bookmaker, lay the same selection on the exchange. The maths guarantees profit regardless of the result.

SNR free bet lay-off: Optimal lay stake = Free Bet × (Back Odds - 1) / (Lay Odds - Commission) £20 SNR free bet, back @ 5.00, lay @ 5.20, 2% commission: Lay stake = 20 × 4 / (5.20 - 0.02×5.20) = 80 / (5.20 - 0.104) = 80 / 5.096 = £15.70 If selection wins: Bookmaker: +£80 (free bet profit, no stake return) Exchange: -£15.70 × (5.20 - 1) = -£65.94 Net: +£14.06 If selection loses: Bookmaker: £0 Exchange: +£15.70 × (1 - 0.02) = +£15.39 Net: +£15.39 Slight difference due to commission — both outcomes profitable.
By laying off, you turn a probabilistic free bet into a guaranteed £14–15 in real cash from a £20 free bet. That's a 70-75% retention rate. The mathematics removes all gambling — it's pure arithmetic.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is a free bet actually worth?

A Stake Not Returned (SNR) free bet is worth approximately (odds-1)/odds of its face value. A £10 free bet at 2/1 is worth about £6.67.

What's the difference between SNR and SR free bets?

SNR (Stake Not Returned) means you only get the profit. SR (Stake Returned) means you also get the stake back. SR free bets are worth their full face value.

How do I use a free bet for guaranteed profit?

Place the free bet on one outcome, then lay (bet against) at an exchange. The difference between back and lay minus commission is your guaranteed profit.

Are free bets really free?

The free bet itself costs nothing, but qualifying usually requires placing a real-money bet first. Factor in qualifying losses when calculating true profit.

What odds should I use for a free bet?

Higher odds = higher value from SNR free bets but more variance. For matched betting, use odds between 3.0-6.0 for the best balance of value and reliability.
