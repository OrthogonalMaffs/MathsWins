WHAT IS OVERROUND?

Every bookmaker builds a profit margin into their odds. They shorten every price slightly so the implied probabilities add up to more than 100%. The amount above 100% is the overround — and it represents the bookmaker's guaranteed edge.

Think of it this way: if a fair coin toss should pay 2.00 on heads and 2.00 on tails (each 50%, totalling 100%), a bookmaker might offer 1.91 on both. Now each implied probability is 52.4%, totalling 104.8%. That extra 4.8% is the overround — the bookmaker's built-in profit margin.
Implied probability = 1 ÷ decimal odds × 100 Example: Liverpool vs Man City Home 2.10 → 1/2.10 = 47.62% Draw 3.40 → 1/3.40 = 29.41% Away 3.50 → 1/3.50 = 28.57% Total implied probability = 105.60% Overround = 105.60% − 100% = 5.60%
The bookmaker has built a 5.6% margin into this market. For every £100 staked across all outcomes in proportion, the bookmaker expects to keep £5.30 and pay out £94.70.

WHY IT MATTERS

The overround tells you exactly how much the bookmaker keeps. You can calculate the expected payout per £1 staked on any market:

Expected payout per £1 = 100 / (100 + overround) At 5% overround: 100/105 = £0.952 per £1 staked At 10% overround: 100/110 = £0.909 per £1 staked At 20% overround: 100/120 = £0.833 per £1 staked
Overround is the betting equivalent of the house edge in a casino. A roulette wheel has a 2.7% house edge. A football match-result market at 5% overround has a roughly similar cost per bet. But some markets — like correct score or first goalscorer — can have overrounds above 30%.
Practical tip: Always check the overround before placing a bet. Two bookmakers might both offer 2.10 on Liverpool, but if one has 104% total book and the other has 108%, the first is giving you fairer value across the market.

OVERROUND ON ACCUMULATORS

This is where bookmakers make serious money. The overround compounds with each leg of an accumulator. The formula for the combined bookmaker edge is:

Combined book = (book&sub1; × book&sub2; × ... × bookₙ) × 100 − 100 Where each book = total implied probability / 100 Example: 5 legs, each with 5% overround (book = 1.05) Combined = 1.05&sup5; × 100 − 100 = 1.2763 × 100 − 100 = 27.6% The bookmaker's edge is 27.6% — not 25%.
Danger: A 10-leg accumulator where each market has just 5% overround gives the bookmaker a combined edge of 62.9%. You are expected to lose 62.9p of every £1 staked. This is worse than almost any casino game. Bookmakers promote accumulators for a reason — they are the most profitable product in the shop.

HOW TO USE THIS INFORMATION

Understanding overround gives you a mathematical framework for making better decisions:

Compare bookmakers: Before placing a bet, check the overround at multiple bookmakers. A market at 102.5% gives you dramatically better value than the same market at 108%. Over hundreds of bets, this difference is the difference between slow losses and near break-even.
Use betting exchanges: Exchanges like Betfair and Smarkets typically have overrounds of 100–102%, compared to 104–110% at traditional bookmakers. You pay a commission on winnings instead, but the net cost is usually lower.
Avoid high-overround markets: Correct score, first goalscorer, and novelty markets often have overrounds above 20%. These are the bookmaker's most profitable products. Stick to match results, Asian handicaps, and over/under totals for the fairest prices.
Think twice about accumulators: Every leg multiplies the bookmaker's edge. If you enjoy accumulators, keep them to 2-3 legs maximum. Beyond that, the compounding overround makes the maths almost impossible to overcome.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is a good overround percentage?

Under 103% is excellent and typically found on major football match-result markets at competitive bookmakers or betting exchanges. 103–106% is good and standard for most mainstream markets. Above 110%, you are paying a significant margin and should consider shopping around for better odds.

How does overround affect accumulators?

Overround compounds with each leg. If each market has 5% overround, a 5-leg accumulator gives the bookmaker a 27.6% edge — not 25%. A 10-leg acca at the same overround gives a 62.9% edge. This is why bookmakers aggressively promote accumulators.

What is the difference between overround and vig?

They are the same concept with different names. Overround is the standard UK and European term. Vig (short for vigorish) or juice is the US term. Both describe the bookmaker's built-in profit margin — the amount by which implied probabilities exceed 100%.

Do betting exchanges have overround?

Exchanges typically have very low overround, usually 100–102%, because prices are set by other bettors rather than a bookmaker. Instead of overround, exchanges charge a commission (usually 2–5%) on net winnings. The total cost to the bettor is generally lower than traditional bookmaker overround.
