HOW THE MATHS WORKS

Inside IR35 — The Umbrella Model

When you work inside IR35, your income passes through an umbrella company. The umbrella deducts its margin (typically 5%), then pays employer NI (13.8% above £9,100) from what remains. Whatever is left becomes your taxable salary — subject to income tax and employee NI, just like any PAYE employee.

Taxable salary = Gross − Umbrella margin − Employer NI

The critical point: employer NI comes out of your gross, not on top of it. This is the single biggest cost of being inside IR35.

Outside IR35 — The Ltd Company Model

Outside IR35, your limited company invoices the client directly. After deducting legitimate business expenses, the company pays corporation tax on its profits. You then extract money as a combination of salary and dividends.

Take-home = Salary (at personal allowance) + Dividends (taxed at lower rates)

The optimal strategy is to take a salary at the personal allowance (£12,570) — no income tax, no employee NI — and take the rest as dividends. Dividend tax rates (8.75% basic, 33.75% higher) are significantly lower than income tax rates.

Why The Gap Is So Large

Two compounding factors create the IR35 gap:

1. Employer NI: At 13.8% above the threshold, this alone costs £12,000–£15,000/year on a typical contractor income. Inside IR35, this comes from your gross. Outside IR35, you only pay employer NI on the small salary portion.

2. Dividend efficiency: Corporation tax (25%) plus dividend tax (8.75% basic rate) gives an effective combined rate of around 31.6% on basic-rate dividends — compared to 40% income tax plus 2% NI for a higher-rate PAYE employee.

IR35 status depends on the working relationship — control, substitution, and mutuality of obligation. The financial difference is substantial, but you cannot simply choose your status. If the working practices look like employment, IR35 applies regardless of what the contract says.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is IR35?

IR35 is tax legislation that determines whether a contractor is genuinely self-employed or effectively an employee. If inside IR35, you're taxed like an employee even though you work through a company.

Who decides my IR35 status?

For public sector and medium/large private sector clients, the client decides. For small private sector clients, you (the contractor) decide. The decision is based on working practices, not the contract alone.

Why does inside IR35 pay so much less?

Two reasons: employer NI (13.8%) is deducted from your gross, and you can't extract profits as dividends (which are taxed at lower rates than salary). The employer NI alone costs roughly £12,000–£15,000 per year.

What's the optimal salary for a Ltd company?

Usually £12,570 (the personal allowance). Below the NI primary threshold so no employee NI, and fully covered by the personal allowance so no income tax. Everything above comes as dividends.

Should I increase my day rate for inside IR35?

Yes — most contractors add 15–25% to their day rate to compensate for the tax difference. This calculator shows you the exact uplift needed.

Can I work outside IR35?

It depends on how you work, not what you want. Key factors: control, substitution rights, and mutuality of obligation. If the client controls when/where/how you work, IR35 likely applies.
