THE FORMULA
Impermanent Loss = 2√r / (1 + r) - 1 where r = price_ratio (new relative price / old relative price) For a pair where one token is stable (USDC): r = (Token A new price / Token A old price) IL is always ≤ 0 (you always underperform holding). IL is symmetrical: 2× up = same IL as 0.5× down. IL is "impermanent" only if price returns to entry level.
The pool constantly sells your winner and buys your loser — that's the rebalancing mechanism of the constant product formula. If ETH doubles, the pool sold half your ETH on the way up. If ETH halves, the pool bought more ETH on the way down. Either way, you'd have been better off holding. The question is whether trading fees compensate for this cost.
