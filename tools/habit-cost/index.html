THE MATHS BEHIND THE NUMBERS

Compound Growth — The Eighth Wonder

When you invest money regularly, you don't just earn returns on what you put in. You earn returns on your returns. This is compound growth, and it's the reason small daily amounts become enormous sums over time.

FV = PMT × ((1 + r)n − 1) / r

Where FV is the future value, PMT is your regular monthly contribution, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the total number of months.

Opportunity Cost

Opportunity cost is what you give up by choosing one thing over another. When you spend £14 on cigarettes, the cost isn't just £14. It's £14 plus everything that £14 could have become if invested. Over decades, that's the difference between a habit and a house deposit.

Why Daily Amounts Feel Small But Aren't

Our brains are terrible at intuiting exponential growth. £4 a day feels like nothing. £1,460 a year feels manageable. £147,000 over 30 years feels impossible. But it's the same money — just viewed through a wider lens. This calculator exists to widen that lens.

We're not your doctor, your mum, or your conscience. We just think you should see the numbers at least once. What you do with them is entirely up to you.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How much does smoking cost per year?

At £14 per pack (20 cigarettes), a pack-a-day smoker spends £5,110 per year. Over 10 years: £51,100. Invested at 7%: £75,602. Over a lifetime: potentially close to £1 million.

What's the opportunity cost of daily coffee?

A £4 daily coffee habit costs £1,460/year. Invested at 7% for 30 years, that becomes £147,000. The coffee isn't the problem — it's what the money could have become.

Is this calculator trying to make me quit?

No. We show the maths. What you do with it is your decision. Some people see these numbers and cut back. Others decide the enjoyment is worth the cost. Both are valid — as long as you've seen the numbers.

Why use 7% growth rate?

7% is the approximate long-term average return of global stock markets after inflation. It's not guaranteed, but it's a widely used benchmark for long-term investment projections.

How do you calculate the invested value?

Future value of a regular monthly investment with compound growth: FV = PMT × ((1+r)n − 1) / r. The same formula banks and pension providers use.
