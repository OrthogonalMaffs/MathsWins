HOW STAMP DUTY WORKS

Progressive Bands, Not a Flat Rate

Stamp duty is often misunderstood. It does not apply as a flat percentage on the entire purchase price. Instead, it works like income tax — different rates apply to different portions (bands) of the price.

If you buy a house for £300,000 in England (standard rates, after April 2025), you do not pay 5% on the full amount. You pay 0% on the first £125,000, then 2% on the next £125,000, then 5% on the remaining £50,000. The total is £5,000 — an effective rate of just 1.67%.

Tax for each band = (Amount in band) × (Band rate)
Total stamp duty = Sum of tax across all bands
Effective rate = Total stamp duty ÷ Purchase price × 100

First-Time Buyer Relief

First-time buyers get a higher nil-rate threshold, meaning you pay nothing on a larger chunk of the price. From April 2025, the first £300,000 is tax-free on properties up to £500,000. This can save you thousands compared to standard rates.

If your property exceeds £500,000, first-time buyer relief is lost entirely and you pay standard rates on the full amount. This cliff edge is important to understand — a property at £499,000 gets relief, but one at £501,000 does not.

The Additional Property Surcharge

If you are buying a second home, holiday home, or buy-to-let investment, a flat surcharge is added on top of standard rates across every band. In England, this is 5% (increased from 3% in October 2024). On a £300,000 property, that is an extra £15,000 on top of the standard £5,000.

Scotland has the highest surcharge at 8%. Wales charges 5%.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How much is stamp duty on a £300,000 house?

For a standard purchase in England (after April 2025): £5,000. For a first-time buyer: £0 on the first £300,000, so £0 total. For an additional property: £5,000 + £15,000 surcharge = £20,000. The amount varies significantly by buyer type.

Do first-time buyers pay stamp duty?

Below certain thresholds, no. From April 2025: nothing on the first £300,000 (on properties up to £500,000). Before April 2025 the nil-rate band was temporarily raised to £425,000. First-time buyer relief can save you up to £6,250.

What is the additional property surcharge?

If you are buying a second home or buy-to-let, you pay an extra 5% on top of standard rates on the ENTIRE purchase price. On a £300,000 property that is an extra £15,000. The surcharge was 3% until October 2024, when it rose to 5%.

Is stamp duty different in Scotland?

Yes. Scotland uses Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT) with different bands and thresholds. The additional property surcharge is 8% in Scotland (vs 5% in England and Wales). First-time buyers in Scotland pay nothing on the first £175,000.

When do I pay stamp duty?

Within 14 days of completion. Your solicitor usually handles the filing and payment. Late payment incurs interest and penalties from HMRC (or Revenue Scotland / Welsh Revenue Authority).

Can I add stamp duty to my mortgage?

Some lenders allow this, but it increases your loan amount and total interest paid over the mortgage term. This calculator shows you the monthly mortgage cost of adding stamp duty — for example, £10,000 added to a 25-year mortgage at 5% costs roughly £58 per month.
