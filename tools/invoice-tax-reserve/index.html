FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How much should I set aside for tax?

As a rule of thumb, 25–30% for basic rate taxpayers, 40–45% for higher rate. This calculator gives the exact figure based on your income.

What if I set aside too much?

Great — you'll have a surplus when your tax bill arrives. Much better than a shortfall. Consider it forced savings.

Should I put the reserve in a savings account?

Yes. A separate savings account earning interest while you wait to pay HMRC is free money. Some banks offer specific tax reserve accounts.

When do I need to pay the tax?

31 January after the end of the tax year. So tax on 2025/26 income (April 2025–April 2026) is due 31 January 2027.

What about VAT?

This calculator covers income tax and NI only. If you're VAT registered (turnover over £90,000), you'll also need to set aside 20% VAT on top.
